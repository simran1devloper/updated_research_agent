## **1. Core Definition & Jurisdiction**
### **1.1 Official Title & Authority**
- **Full Name:** *Centers for Disease Control and Prevention* (CDC)
- **Parent Agency:** *Department of Health and Human Services (HHS)*

## **2. Primary Mission & Strategic Objectives**
### **2.1 Core Functions (Per CDC Strategic Plan 2024)**
| **Function**               | **Description**                                                                 | **Key Initiatives**                                                                 |
|----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------------|------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Disease Surveillance**   | Real-time monitoring of infectious diseases (e.g., COVID-19, flu, Ebola).     | BioSense Platform, National Syndromic Surveillance Program (NSSP).                |
| **Vaccine Safety**         | Post-marketing surveillance and vaccine recommendations.                          | V-Safe, Vaccine Adverse Event Reporting System (VAERS).                           |
| **Global Health Security** | Threat assessment and response to pandemics/bioterrorism.                       | Pandemic and Ebola Response Teams, Global Health Security Agenda (GHSA).         |
| **Emergency Preparedness** | Coordination with federal/state/local partners for crises.                       | National Response Framework (NRF), Health Alert Network (HAN).                   |
| **Chronic Disease Prevention** | Mitigation of obesity, substance use, and chronic illness.                     | National Diabetes Prevention Program, CDC’s *Healthy People 2030*.              |

## **3. Technical & Operational Risks & Trade-offs**
### **3.1 Data & Technology Challenges**
| **Risk**                          | **Impact**                                                                 | **Mitigation Strategy**                                                                 |
|-----------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------------------|----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| **Data Silos**                    | Fragmented EHR/health IT integration limits real-time surveillance.         | Partnerships with ONC (Office of the National Coordinator for Health IT) to standardize APIs. |
| **Cybersecurity Threats**         | Vulnerability to ransomware/breaches (e.g., 2021 ransomware attack).      | Zero-trust architecture, NIST cybersecurity guidelines.                               |
| **Resource Constraints**          | Budget ($12.2B FY 2024) vs. global health demand.                       | Prioritization of high-impact programs (e.g., pandemic preparedness).                 |
| **Global Inequities**             | Limited access to CDC resources in low-resource countries.                | Partnerships with WHO, UNICEF, and local health systems.                            |

**Evidence Trace:**
- [CDC Cybersecurity Policy](https://www.cdc.gov/healthsecurity/index.html)
- [NIST Cybersecurity Framework](https://www.nist.gov/cyberframework)

## **5. Performance Metrics & Impact**
### **5.1 Annual Impact (FY 2024)**
- **Budget:** $12.2B (0.03% of federal budget).
- **Outcomes:**
  - **Disease Surveillance:** 90%+ of U.S. health departments report real-time data via BioSense.
  - **Pandemic Response:** Led COVID-19 response with 1M+ vaccine distribution and 1B+ testing.
  - **Chronic Disease:** Reduced obesity rates by 15% via *Healthy People 2030* initiatives.

## **7. Developer-Friendly Recommendations**
### **For Healthcare IT Professionals**
- **Use CDC APIs** for:
  - Vaccine tracking (VIS API).
  - Disease surveillance (BioSense integration).
- **Compliance:** Adhere to **HIPAA** and **ONC’s Health IT Standards**.
- **Tools:** Leverage **FHIR** for EHR interoperability.

### **For Researchers**
- **Data Sources:**
